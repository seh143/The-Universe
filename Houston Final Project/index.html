Welcome to a personal tour of the universe and the celestial bodies within it.

We have planets, stars, blackholes, and more for you to learn about. I hope you enjoy reading about our solar system as well as exoplanets and stars. The star life cycle is a personal favorite of mine, especially during the last stages of their life. The universe is ever expanding and forming new stars and planets all the time, as well as destroying itself just to create new objects in our night sky.

Make sure to check out our fun facts about the Moons of our solar system!!!!

  • Pluto is 100% a planet. Viva la Pluto.
  • Mercury has the longest day length, but the shortest year.
  • Jupiter is the largest and most massive planet.
  • An Astonomical Unit is the mean distance from the earth to the sun.
  • There are four types of exoplanets: Terrestrial, Super-Earth, Neptune-Like, and Gas Giant
  • Our solar system is about 4,500 million years old.
  • Supernova clouds expand at a rate of 15,000 to 40,000 kilometers per second.
